Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

Rhetoric, technology, and ethics shape how humans create and share ideas. Rhetoric is the key to making a sentence sound beautiful. Technology provides a lot of productivity, as it can bring those crazy thoughts inside people's minds to life. I have applied rhetorical techniques in my portfolio by carefully choosing words and visual elements that strengthen the impact of my work. Additionally, I have explored how different technologies enhance my creative process, making my designs more dynamic and efficient. Ethics is the most formal concept here; it represents a lot. As creators, we have to make sure we don’t copy from AI or engage in any other form of plagiarism. I have taken extra steps to ensure my work remains original by thoroughly researching and verifying sources. Understanding ethics also helps me make responsible design choices that consider social and cultural impacts.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

Leverage Design Principles means using established design concepts and guidelines effectively to create better solutions, products, or experiences. We may use those concept to help us improve on our own design.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

That is absolute the greatest part in our class. All designer have many creative idea in their mind. And it is nice to share with peers. Unforunately I didn't join much workshop compare to others. But when I engage with the class, I feel no press and very comfortable to talking with others.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

It is imporant to keep practice as a creator. Especially for those fantastic applications. For me, I have get in touch with photoshop when I was in middle school, but after few years without PS. I almost forget everything except some basic skills. So it is important to keep practice with those apps.

5. Learn to present your work.

A good design consists not only of a perfect final product but also requires the creator to present it effectively. Sometimes, your audience may not immediately understand what you are trying to communicate, so you must ensure you fully explain your work to them. A strong presentation can sometimes be more important than the design itself. I have learned to highlight key aspects of my work and structure my explanations clearly to engage my audience. In my portfolio, I have also focused on developing my storytelling skills to make my presentations more compelling. Being able to confidently present my work has given me a stronger sense of professionalism and has improved my ability to communicate my ideas effectively.

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

This semester past ⅔. I feel like I rely on clear communication and the right digital tools. All my word, font, or color has a purpose. I notice how important it is to follow ethical practice. I want to avoid plagiarism. And I also want to respect intellectual and cultural work from others. This focus helps me create more responsible designs.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

I used design principles like proportion, alignment, and color theory. These ideas guide me through my busy scheduleThey can turn a rushed project into a polished one. This blend helps my work stay clear and balanced. But I do have a bad habbit that I always turn in my work right before the deadline.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

My workload is heavy, but I still share ideas with classmates. Feedback helps me improve my concepts. I can also learn new methods when I see other people’s work. Even a short workshop gives me more energy. It shows me that design grows best in a group. I see how open communication helps designer on their work.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

I often revise my work. I edit both digital designs and written proposals. Each revision fixes small mistakes. My many assignments push me to be strategic. Even a small edit can make a big difference.

5. Learn to present your work.

Good design is not enough on its own. I also need to explain it well. A clear presentation shows my key decisions. It also shows the thought behind them. This step can make simple projects stand out.
